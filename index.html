Presense

As a PhD candidate at Carnegie Mellon University's McWilliams Center for Cosmology, I work on data-driven astrophysical projects with data from observational sky-surveys and state-of-art cosmological simulations.

Utilizing the astronomical data from the GAIA mission, I am currently developing software using the kernel density estimation and the Poisson statistics of stellar number counts to identify stellar density peaks to search for possibly hiding globular clusters. Click here :)

Past

I have done projects on studying supermassive black holes with cosmological hydrodynamic simulations: (1) running new multiple constrained simulations to test different black hole seeding scenarios, (2) analyzing black holes in the 5000 resolved galaxies in the BlueTides simulation.

As a master student in physics at National Taiwan University, I have modified a numerical fluid solver to cope with a different dark matter model to study computational cosmology. Click here :)
