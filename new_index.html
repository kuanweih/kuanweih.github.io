About

As a machine learning research engineer with a PhD in Physics, I bring a unique perspective to the field of artificial intelligence. My journey began with a deep curiosity about the fundamental laws that govern our universe, which naturally evolved into exploring how we can encode intelligence into computational systems.

My research philosophy is rooted in the principle of elegance—finding simple, beautiful solutions to complex problems. Just as physics seeks to understand the universe through fundamental equations, I believe machine learning should strive for models that are both powerful and interpretable.

Currently, I focus on developing novel neural architectures inspired by physical principles, working at the intersection of deep learning, statistical mechanics, and computational physics.
