PhD Research Project (in progress)

Searching for Possibly Missing Globular Clusters Associated with the Milky Way Satellites

There may have been more globular clusters (GCs) lurking around the Milky Way satellite galaxies. Using the kernel density estimation and the Poisson statistics of stellar number counts to identify stellar density peaks, I have been developing a program to search for possibly hiding GCs. Selecting useful data from more than 1.7 billion astronomical objects in the GAIA mission, I have conducted the searching program on the targeted area near 56 Milky Way dwarf galaxies. More detail will come out soon :)

Hyperpartisan News Classification

There is a lot of information that we are exposed to every day. Some are neutral. Some are not. This task is to determine whether or not a news article text follows a hyperpartisan argumentation. This topic originates from the SemEval 2019 competetion and we use the winning entry of 82% accuracy as the baseline model. We have improved the model with an accuracy of 85% by (1) using ELMo to generate sentence level embeddings, (2) augmenting dataset via back-translation, (3) using CNNs hierarchically to generate a document level representation from word-embeddings. PhD Research Project

The Growth of Supermassive Black Holes in Cosmological Hydrodynamic Simulations

There are different scenarios in the formation of supermassive black holes (SMBHs). However, it remains unknown due to the lack of observation in the early universe. Moreover, running multiple cosmological hydrodynamic simulations are too expensive to examine the scenarios. With the help of the constrained Gaussian realization, we can reduce the computational demand by a factor of ~20000. We then have conducted multiple cosmological simulations with more than ~1000 CPU hours to study the growth of SMBHs with different seeding scenarios. The result is published in [3].

PhD Research Project

Establishing Supermassive Black Hole-Galaxy Relations in BlueTides Simulation

Observed in the current nearby universe, the scaling relations between the supermassive black holes (SMBHs) and their host galaxies tell the connection between them. What about the earlier universe? We use 5000 resolved galaxies in the BlueTides simulation, which contains ~700 billion particles, to establish scaling relations with the mass of SMBHs and their host galaxy properties. Interestingly, the scaling relations we found in the early universe are consistent with existing ones from observations. The result is published in [2].

MS Thesis

Numerically Solving the Schrodinger Equation in the Hydrodynamic Form

Quantum wave dark matter (ψDM) has predicted the large-scale structure consistent with the cold dark matter but a completely different small-scale structure. However, ψDM is too computational expensive due to its quantum nature. Treating the ψDM as quantum fluid, I have modified MPI software to try to solve the Schrodinger-Poisson equation in the hydrodynamic form by adding numerical diffusion, changing the mathematical form, and regularizing the flux term. My 1st Research Project

The Structure of the Universe with Quantum Wave Dark Matter (ψDM)

The cold dark matter (CDM) has succeeded in many aspects to explain the large-scale structure of the universe. However, it is not perfect when it comes to small scales. Governed by different physics principles, the quantum wave dark matter (ψDM) has predicted consistent large-scale structures with the CDM. We find that ψDM can further predict different small-scale structures to handle the issues CDM cannot deal with. The result is published in [1].
